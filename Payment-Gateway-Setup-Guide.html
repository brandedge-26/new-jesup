Payment Gateway Setup Guide

Step-by-step instructions to obtain Stripe and PayPal credentials  |  Share with your developer once done

1. Stripe  Card Payments
  • 1
    Create a Stripe Account Go to stripe.com and click Start now. Sign up with your email address and complete email verification.
  • 2
    Complete Business Activation (for live payments) After logging in, click Activate your account in the top banner. Fill in your business details, bank account info, and personal ID. This is required to receive real money. You can skip this step during development/testing.
  • 3
    Go to the API Keys page In the Stripe Dashboard, click Developers (top-right) → then click API keys in the left menu.
  • 4
    Copy your keys You will see two keys on this page. Copy both and send them to your developer.
Publishable Key
Starts with pk_test_ (test) or pk_live_ (live)
Used on the website frontend. Safe to share with developer.
Secret Key
Starts with sk_test_ (test) or sk_live_ (live)
Used on the server. Never share publicly. Send only to your developer privately.
Note: Use test mode keys first (toggle at the top of Stripe Dashboard). Switch to live keys only when you are ready to accept real payments. Test card number: 4242 4242 4242 4242, any future expiry, any 3-digit CVC.
2. PayPal  PayPal Payments
  • 1
    Create a PayPal Business Account Go to paypal.com → click Sign Up → choose Business Account. Complete registration with your business email and details.
  • 2
    Go to PayPal Developer Dashboard Visit developer.paypal.com and log in with your PayPal business account.
  • 3
    Create an App Go to Apps & Credentials in the top menu. Make sure you are on the Sandbox tab for testing. Click Create App, give it any name (e.g., "My Store"), and click Create App again.
  • 4
    Copy your credentials After creating the app, you will see your credentials on the same page. Copy both and send them to your developer.
Client ID
Long string of letters and numbers
Used on both frontend and backend. Safe to share with developer.
Client Secret
Click "Show" to reveal it
Used only on the server. Never share publicly. Send only to your developer privately.
Note: Use Sandbox credentials for testing. When ready to go live, switch to the Live tab in the developer dashboard and create/copy your live app credentials.
3. What to Send Your Developer
Stripe Publishable Key
pk_test_... or pk_live_...
Stripe Secret Key
sk_test_... or sk_live_...
PayPal Client ID
From PayPal Developer Dashboard → Your App
PayPal Client Secret
From PayPal Developer Dashboard → Your App → Show
Security tip: Send credentials privately — via a secure message or encrypted email. Do not share them over regular SMS, WhatsApp, or in screenshots that could be forwarded.
